Concept V2 — Crew Quarters

Starship Crew Quarters

Cosy personal quarters · sloped hull glass for star-watching · couch + bed · anchor-based movement · soft bright palette

The room becomes somewhere you live, not just sit. The defining move, borrowed as a quality (not a copy) from classic sci-fi interiors: the outer hull wall leans steeply inward, and the windows sit in the slope — so from the couch the starfield hangs above you, and lying on the bed you look straight up into space. Two zones — a sunken-feel lounge and a raised sleeping alcove — each with their own glass and their own perspective on the room. Cream and oatmeal surfaces, a muted plum carpet, and warm indirect coves replace the dark charcoal shell.

IP guardrail. We take the reference's qualities: sloped glazing, rounded furniture, warm indirect light, lived-in softness. We deliberately avoid its signatures: no paired oval windows with a horizontal divider bar, no franchise UI colors/layouts, no delta or chevron motifs, no matching set dressing. Window shapes, beam profiles, and palette below are original.

01Section — glass in the slope

Side section through the lounge. The hull face rises from a 0.75 m sill at roughly 55°, carrying the main glazing, then returns to a flat ceiling. The couch tucks under the slope; a seated viewer's natural upward gaze lands in the middle of the starfield.

Ceiling drops to 2.5 m (from 3.3) — quarters should feel snug, not grand. The slope gives ~1.9 m of glass height without the room feeling exposed.

02Plan — two zones, four perspectives

6.4 × 5.2 m. Lounge on the left under the main glazing; sleeping alcove on the right, raised one step, with its own pane. Marked anchors are teleport targets — each one is a deliberately different view of the same room, which is what makes a small space feel inhabited.

Room is deliberately just larger than a typical playspace: anchors handle zone-to-zone hops; real walking covers everything within a zone.

03The window wall from the couch

Three rounded trapezoid panes in the lounge slope, one wide pane over the sleep alcove — an original rhythm, not the reference's paired ovals. Planters and books live on the sill; the cove light grazes up the slope between panes.

All panes share one star shader (parallax layers + twinkle + slow nebula mode) rendered on quads behind the apertures — replaces the 180 dot cubes and the particle box, and fixes the direction-flip.

04Palette — soft bright

Warm neutrals carry the room; the plum carpet grounds it; amber and teal shrink to small accents. Warm-white cove light does the atmospheric work the amber panels used to do.

Brighter room, same restfulness: overall luminance kept ~1 stop below "daylight interior", windows ~1.5 stops darker than walls so stars always read. All static geometry lightmapped; one runtime light total.

06Movement — anchors + real walking

Real head-tracked walking stays exactly as it is today. Zone hops use teleport anchors, which keeps the no-artificial-motion comfort rule intact: you never see translation.
